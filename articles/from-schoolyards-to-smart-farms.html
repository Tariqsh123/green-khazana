From Schoolyards to Smart Farms: How Young Agripreneurs Are Powering the Green Economy

By Prof. Nadeem Ahmed Faraz

By 2026, agripreneurship at schools has evolved into a global movement shaping climate resilience, employment, and sustainable economic growth. With climate change threatening food systems and livelihoods, schools are preparing students not just as learners, but as solution-driven agripreneurs. According to UNESCO, education-based sustainability programs now reach over 300,000 students globally, integrating climate action, entrepreneurship, and financial literacy into everyday learning.

Initiatives such as the Eco-Schools Programme, active in more than 70 countries and 51,000 schools, enable students to lead green initiatives including school farms, composting, water conservation, and climate-smart agriculture. Meanwhile, FAO-backed youth programs like Cool Agripreneur introduce students to modern farming technologies, agribusiness models, and carbon-smart practices, directly linking agriculture with the green economy.

Through kidspreurship, students learn how small agricultural ideas can generate income, create employment, and build long-term prosperity. Early exposure to agribusiness nurtures an entrepreneurial mindset, strengthens financial literacy, and inspires careers aligned with sustainability. Agripreneurship at schools proves a powerful truth: when young minds grow green ideas, they cultivate a future of resilience, dignity, and shared global prosperity.
